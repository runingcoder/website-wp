Role models

Absolute legends in their own fields of ultra running and running of any kind, just not the stealing kind.
Kilian Jornet
The best against the gravity

Resting as it were seven hours a night, Kilian Jornet appears nearly superhuman. His resting pulse is amazingly moo at 33 beats per miniature, compared with the normal man's 60 per miniature or an athlete's 40 per miniature. He breathes more effectively than normal individuals as well, taking in more oxygen per breath, and he features a much quicker recuperation time after work out as his body rapidly breaks down lactic corrosive – the corrosive in muscles that causes torment after work out.

Courtney Dauwalter
The humblest one

“If each person’s maximum capability is plotted as an individual dot on a graph, comparing men and women would look like two shotgun blasts where the average of the male blast is slightly higher than that of the female. But, identify who signed up for a particular race, then throw in all the factors that can move those dots a little up or a little down on the specific day the race is held — from training prep to race-day decisions and motivations — and a particular woman might well win outright."

Eliud Kipchoge
First sub 2 marathoner

“In the marathon, the first half is just a normal run. At 15 kilometers, 20 kilometers, everybody is still going to be there. Where the marathon starts is after 30 kilometers. That’s where you feel pain everywhere in your body. The muscles are really aching, and only the most prepared and well-organized athlete is going to do well after that. I’ll go with the pace, but after 30 kilometers, I’ll change to my own pace. And if you’re ready to follow me, then we can go together.”
